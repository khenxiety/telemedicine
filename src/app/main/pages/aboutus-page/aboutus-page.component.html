HISTORY

Telemedicine is a health website linked to Telemedicine Kit hardware, which is the output of a capstone project that is a graduation requirement for Batangas State University’s Electronics Engineering students. This capstone project yielded a compact telemedicine kit with cloud storage and website access in order to address the remote diagnostic needs of patients, including health diagnosis, data storage, and forwarding. The system will consist of several biomedical sensors integrated into a microcontroller and then a microprocessor. The biomedical sensors integrated into the kit include a temperature sensor, an electrocardiogram (ECG), a pulse oximeter, a camera, and a pneumograph. The kit can record all assessments and care given to the patient by the responders and determine any threats to the patient’s life. With this information being forwarded and accessed from the cloud, the physician or hospital analyzing the data can summon more advanced medical care as needed or prepare the hospital for receiving the patient
